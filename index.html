Seongmin Kim

Hello, I'm Seongmin Kim (Student Member, IEEE) who is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in electrical engineering with the Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST), Ulsan, South Korea, under the supervision of Prof. Seungjoon Yang in the Signal Processing Laboratory. I also received the B.E. degree in electrical and electronic engineering in 2024 and the M.S. degree in electrical engineering in 2026, both from the University of Ulsan, Ulsan, South Korea, where I was advised by Prof. Kanghyun Jo at the Intelligent Systems Laboratory.

My research interests include artificial intelligence and computer vision. My current research focuses on representation learning, world models, Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA), optimal transport, and partial information decomposition.

Mathematical Analysis of High-Frequency Spatial Attention for Facial Expression Recognition
Seongin Kim and Kanghyun Jo
HSI, 2025  (Best Student Paper)
bibtex

Explained the performance improvement of high-frequency spatial attention in facial expression recognition by interpreting singular value interactions through PCA and empirically validating the low distribution discrepancy between feature maps and high-frequency components using projection distance.

Spatial attention network with high frequency component for facial expression recognition
Seongin Kim and Kanghyun Jo
IW-FCV, 2024  (Excellent Student Research Paper)
bibtex

Improved facial expression recognition performance by implementing an attention mechanism that compares the singular values of high-frequency facial features and CNN spatial feature maps.
